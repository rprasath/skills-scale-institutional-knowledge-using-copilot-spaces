# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Product Manager** on acceptance criteria and priority
- Works with **QA/Testing** on test strategy and quality gates
- Works with **UX Designer** on design specifications and usability
- Coordinates with **Release Manager** on deployment readiness

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Project Manager** on timeline and delivery planning
- Works with **Developers** on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- Works with **UX Designer** on user needs and feature specifications
- Partners with **Business Analyst** on requirements gathering

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Product Manager** on priorities and scope
- Facilitates communication between **Developers**, **QA/Testing**, and stakeholders
- Coordinates with **Scrum Master** on team dynamics and process adherence
- Partners with **Release Manager** on deployment schedules and go-live coordination

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered experiences and interfaces aligned with project objectives and customer needs. They translate user research and product requirements into design specifications that guide implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather requirements from Product Manager and stakeholders
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Develop and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure design fidelity in implementation
- Conduct usability testing and iterate based on feedback
- Document design decisions and accessibility considerations

### Goals
- Deliver user experiences that meet customer needs and business goals
- Ensure consistency across all touchpoints
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Enable Developers to implement designs efficiently

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Design specifications and design system documentation
- Collaboration with Developers during implementation
- Usability testing sessions and feedback synthesis

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Product Manager** on user needs and feature specifications
- Partners with **Developers** on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- Collaborates with **QA/Testing** on acceptance criteria and usability validation
- Shares findings with **Business Analyst** to inform requirements refinement

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Agile Coaches) facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove team impediments, and ensure adherence to Agile practices. They enable self-organizing teams and support continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove impediments blocking the team
- Coach the team on Agile principles and best practices
- Monitor team health, velocity, and capacity
- Support Product Manager and Project Manager in backlog refinement
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable the team to self-organize and maintain a sustainable pace
- Increase delivery predictability and team effectiveness
- Reduce process friction and impediments
- Support team well-being and professional growth

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of Agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retros)
- One-on-ones with team members to understand blockers
- Coaching feedback and process improvement suggestions
- Metrics and velocity reports

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Project Manager** to coordinate timelines and dependency management
- Supports **Developers** by removing impediments and creating psychological safety
- Partners with **Product Manager** on backlog readiness and refinement
- Collaborates with team on continuous improvement action items

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and communicate release activities. They ensure smooth, controlled deployments that minimize risk and maximize stakeholder confidence. They serve as the single point of coordination across development, QA, operations, and support.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain release plans and deployment schedules
- Coordinate with Development, QA, and Operations on readiness
- Maintain release documentation, notes, and known issues
- Coordinate communication with stakeholders and support teams
- Plan and execute rollback procedures if needed
- Conduct post-deployment verification and gather feedback

### Goals
- Execute smooth, predictable releases with minimal risk
- Ensure all stakeholders understand what is being released and when
- Enable rapid issue resolution and rollback if necessary
- Build confidence in the deployment process

### Typical Communication
- Release notes and deployment schedules
- Pre-release checklists and readiness reviews
- Incident communication and status updates
- Post-deployment retrospectives and metrics

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Developers** and **QA/Testing** to validate release readiness
- Coordinates with **Project Manager** on timeline and communication plan
- Communicates with stakeholders and support teams on deployment windows
- Partners with operations on deployment execution and monitoring

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather, analyze, and document business requirements. They translate business needs into actionable acceptance criteria and functional specifications that guide development. They serve as a bridge between stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering sessions
- Document business requirements and user stories
- Develop acceptance criteria and functional specifications
- Clarify ambiguities and validate understanding across teams
- Support refinement sessions and backlog prioritization
- Ensure traceability from business needs to delivered solutions

### Goals
- Ensure that delivered solutions address real business problems
- Reduce rework and misalignment through clear requirements
- Enable fast decision-making with validated information
- Improve team efficiency by clarifying scope early

### Typical Communication
- Requirement documents and user story specifications
- Acceptance criteria documentation
- Clarification questions and validation meetings
- Traceability matrices linking requirements to deliverables

### Collaboration Points
- Works with stakeholders and **Product Manager** on business needs and priorities
- Partners with **Developers** to ensure feasibility and technical accuracy
- Collaborates with **UX Designer** on user needs and design specifications
- Supports **QA/Testing** by providing clear acceptance criteria
- Assists **Project Manager** in scope management and change control

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA and Testing professionals validate that delivered solutions meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They design test strategies, execute tests, and help identify and prevent defects.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test plans and test cases based on acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Report bugs and validate fixes
- Conduct regression testing and smoke tests
- Support release readiness validation
- Contribute to quality metrics and continuous improvement

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality features with minimal production defects
- Reduce time spent on rework and remediation
- Build confidence in the readiness of releases
- Enable fast, risk-aware decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and status dashboards
- Release readiness reports
- Quality metrics and trend analysis

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Developers** on test strategy and acceptance criteria
- Collaborates with **Product Manager** to validate feature acceptance
- Partners with **UX Designer** on usability and user experience validation
- Supports **Release Manager** on go-live readiness
- Works with **Business Analyst** to validate requirements are met

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are business leaders, sponsors, and decision-makers who define project priorities, provide resources, and approve major decisions. They represent customer or business interests in the project.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success metrics
- Provide project sponsorship and secure resources
- Approve scope changes and major decisions
- Provide timely feedback and direction
- Remove organizational blockers
- Accept or reject deliverables

### Goals
- Achieve business outcomes and deliver value
- Ensure alignment with organizational strategy
- Minimize risk and manage stakeholder expectations
- Enable timely decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Monthly or quarterly status updates
- Risk escalations and impact assessments
- Approval of scope changes and resource requests

### Collaboration Points
- Works with **Project Manager** on planning, risks, and status
- Reviews deliverables with **Product Manager** for acceptance
- Participates in retrospectives and feedback sessions
- Provides input to **Business Analyst** on requirements and priorities
