## OctoAcme Project Management Processes: Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, five-phase project lifecycle designed to maximize customer value while maintaining clear ownership and data-driven decision-making. Projects begin with **Initiation**, where a Project One-pager validates business need, aligns stakeholders, and establishes success metrics. Once approved, teams move into **Planning**, breaking work into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs, acceptance criteria, and identified dependencies. The **Execution** phase focuses on delivery through daily standups, weekly syncs, and iteration-based development with strict quality gates. After development, teams conduct **Release & Deployment** using standardized checklists and rollback procedures to minimize production risk. Finally, **Retrospectives & Continuous Improvement** capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements, closing the feedback loop.

Roles are clearly defined: **Project Managers** coordinate delivery, manage timelines and risks, and ensure stakeholder alignment; **Product Managers** define deliverables, prioritize backlogs, and measure outcomes; **Developers** implement features, write tests, and collaborate on design; and **QA/Testing** validates quality against acceptance criteria. Communication is structured—daily standups for progress and blockers, weekly strategic syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates provide visibility and ensure risks are escalated through clear channels.

Quality assurance is deeply embedded: small pull requests, thorough code review, robust automated and manual tests, and CI security checks form a foundation for confidence and repeatable outcomes. Metrics like velocity and burndown are tracked; risk registers and dashboards are kept current.

All knowledge and process artifacts—including the Project Charter, Roadmaps, Backlogs, Risk Registers, Retrospectives, and process templates—are versioned in the `docs/` folder. This approach ensures new and existing team members have a unified, discoverable entry point to OctoAcme best practices, reducing onboarding time and promoting consistency.
